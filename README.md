# Decoding the 2026 Tamil Nadu Assembly Election

### Understanding one of the biggest political shifts in recent Tamil Nadu history

## The Question

In 2026, TVK contested its first Tamil Nadu Assembly election and won **108 of 234 seats**.

This project explores **how** that happened: where votes shifted, which regions changed, and what the numbers reveal about Tamil Nadu's new political landscape.

---

## Key Findings

### A Historic Realignment

* **163 of 234 constituencies flipped** between 2021 and 2026.
* A **70% flip rate**, far beyond what is typical in competitive elections.
* The 2026 map looked fundamentally different from 2021.

### TVK Drew Support From Everywhere

TVK's gains came from across the political spectrum:

* DMK lost 93 flipped seats, 65 of which went to TVK.
* AIADMK lost 44 flipped seats, 26 to TVK.
* INC lost 14 flipped seats, 11 to TVK.

Rather than replacing a single party, TVK absorbed support from multiple voter bases simultaneously.

### The Shift Started in Cities

| Region        | Flip Rate | TVK Vote Share |
| ------------- | --------- | -------------- |
| Chennai Metro | 94%       | 47%            |
| Kongu         | 82%       | 35%            |
| North         | 73%       | 33%            |
| South         | 66%       | 34%            |
| Central       | 63%       | 31%            |
| Delta         | 46%       | 33%            |

Chennai saw the strongest disruption, while the Delta region remained comparatively stable, suggesting the shift began in urban areas before spreading statewide.

### Vote Share Transformation

| Party  | 2021 | 2026 |
| ------ | ---- | ---- |
| DMK    | 38%  | 24%  |
| AIADMK | 34%  | 21%  |
| TVK    | —    | 35%  |

The combined DMK–AIADMK vote share fell from 72% to 45%, with much of that decline captured by TVK.

### Broad-Based Appeal

TVK won:

* **23 of 44 SC-reserved seats**
* **1 of 2 ST-reserved seats**

Its performance in reserved constituencies suggests support that extended beyond a narrow demographic or regional base.

### Competitive Elections

* Average victory margin fell from **22,871** votes in 2021 to **16,784** in 2026.
* **61 constituencies** were decided by fewer than 5,000 votes.

TVK's victories came in highly competitive contests rather than uncontested races.

---

## Conclusion

Tamil Nadu 2026 was not a typical swing election—it was a political realignment.

A new party entered the field and attracted support across regions, communities, and existing party lines, reshaping a system long dominated by two major parties.

The clearest indicator is the **70% constituency flip rate**, a number that reflects just how dramatically the state's political map changed in five years.
